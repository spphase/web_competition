the Tianyi Pavilion (Ningbo)

Located in the urban area of ​​Ningbo, Zhejiang,the Tianyi Pavilion is the earliest private collection in China. It is also the oldest library in Asia and one of the world's three largest family libraries.The Tianyi Pavilion covers an area of ​​26,000 square meters and was built in the middle of the Ming Dynasty. the Dram Tower (Xi’an)

the Dram Tower (Xi’an)

the Dram Tower is located at the southern end of the North Gate of the West Street in Xi'an City. It faces the Bell Tower in the east and was built in the 13th year of Hongwu Zhu Yuanzhang. It is four years older than the Bell Tower and has a history of 625 years. The 38th year of Emperor Kangxi and the Qing Emperor Qianlong were rebuilt twice. Upstairs, the original big drum, one day beat the drums, so it is called "Drum Tower." After years of vicissitudes of life, the giant drums have long since disappeared, but the Yugu Tower stands tall. It is one of the largest drum towers in China.

the Eastern Tower of Foguang Temple (Wutai Mountain)

the Eastern Tower of Foguang Temple (Wutai Mountain)

the Eastern Tower of Foguang Temple is a world cultural heritage. It is located in Foguang New Village, Wutai County, Shanxi Province. The main hall of the temple is the East Hall. It was built in the eleventh year of the Tang Dynasty, in 857 AD. The Foguang Temple Hall is now the largest existing Tang Dynasty wood structure in China and the second early wooden structure (after the Nanchan Temple Hall in Wutai County). It broke the assertion of Japanese scholars that there was no Tang Dynasty and its previous wooden structures on the Chinese land, so it was hailed by the architect Liang Sicheng as "China's first national treasure." The temple's wooden hall in the Tang Dynasty, colorful sculptures, murals, ink book inscriptions, Jin Jianwenshu Hall, Wei Tang Tomb Tower, Tang Shijing Building, etc. are all precious cultural relics with high historical and artistic value.

the Jin Memorial Temple

The Jin Memorial Temple, originally known as Jin Wangxi (Tang Shuzhen), was built to commemorate the Jin (汾) king and the mother. Located on the bank of Jinshui Mountain in the southwest of Taiyuan City, Shanxi Province, there are dozens of ancient buildings in the city. The environment is elegant and comfortable, and the scenery is beautiful and beautiful. It is famous for its majestic buildings and superb statues. It is the only precious historical and cultural heritage integrating ancient Chinese ritual architecture, gardens, sculptures, murals and inscription art. It is also the center of architecture, gardening and sculpture in the world. Difficult old springs, maids, and the Madonna are known as "three gongs."

the Dwellings in Yi County，Anhui Province

the Dwellings in Yi County，Anhui Province

The two ancient dwelling houses in Xidi and Hongcun, Yixian County, Anhui Province have been included in the World Cultural Heritage with their well-preserved traditional features. This is the second time that the natural and cultural landscapes in the Huangshan Scenic Area have been registered in the World Cultural Heritage Catalogue. The second city in Beijing with more than two world heritage sites, and the world's first time to include dwellings in the World Heritage List. Xidi and Hongcun Ancient Residences are located in Huangshan Scenic Area in the county of Anhui Province in eastern China. Xidi and Hongcun are the two most representative ancient villages in the southern part of Anhui. They are famous for their pastoral scenery, preserved village form, exquisite craftsmanship and rich historical and cultural connotations.

the Chengde Mountain Resort

Chengde Mountain Resort, the ancient Chinese imperial palace, the emperor of the Qing Dynasty to escape the heat and deal with government affairs. Located in the north of the urban area of ​​Chengde City, Hebei Province. It was built in 1703 and has been completed in the Kangxi, Yongzheng and Qianlong dynasties. It is also known as the four famous gardens of China with the national key cultural relics protection unit, the Summer Palace, the Humble Administrator's Garden and the Lingering Garden. the Fujian Earth Building

the Fujian Earth Building, including the Weinan Tulou and some Hakka Earth Buildings, has a total of about 3,000. It is usually referred to as the use of unprocessed raw soil in the southwestern part of the country, and the construction of a large-scale building consisting of a group of sturdy and defensive walls, shaped like a flying saucer, scattered between the mountains and the green water. The main distribution areas are in the southwest mountainous area of ​​Fujian, China, where the Hakkas and Minnans live in the border areas of Fujian, Jiangxi, and Guangdong, including Chenzhou, which is dominated by southern Fujianese, and Longyan, where the southern Fujianese and Hakkas are half-occupied. Fujian Tulou is a unique and large-scale residential area in the world, and is known as the treasure of the traditional Han Chinese residence.
